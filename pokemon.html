Pokémon, an electronic game series from Nintendo, first launched in Japan in 1996 as Pokémon Green and Pokémon Red. The franchise gained popularity in the United States and worldwide, with two titles introduced in 1998 for the Game Boy consoles, known as Red and Blue. In the games, players assume the role of Pokémon trainers, obtaining monsters and developing them to battle other Pokémon. So far there have been a total of 135 Pokemon games, with 1164 different species of Pokemon.
Stats: Most Successful Pokemon Games
With a global sales volume of over 31.38 million units, Pokémon Red/Green/Blue was the franchise's best-selling video game worldwide as of December 2024. The first full Pokémon game release on Nintendo Switch, Pokémon Sword/Shield, came in second place with 26.6 million units. Pokémon Scarlet/Violet, the most recent game on the Switch, has sold approximately 26.38 million units worldwide.
